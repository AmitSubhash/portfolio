Amit Subhash

Amit Subhash is an incoming PhD student in Neuroengineering at Indiana University. His research focuses on Diffuse Optical Tomography (DOT) for neonatal brain imaging, combining Monte Carlo photon transport (MCX, iso2mesh, NIRFASTer, NeuroDOT) with modern deep learning (PyTorch, JAX) toward real-time, contrast-agnostic functional brain imaging in the NICU.

He previously completed an MS in Data Science. His work spans forward and inverse solvers, domain-randomized segmentation pipelines, HPC-scale training on the Indiana University Big Red 200 cluster, and the agentic research infrastructure that orchestrates it all.
