About

I am Amit Subhash, an incoming PhD student in Neuroengineering at Indiana University. I previously completed an MS in Data Science.

My research focuses on Diffuse Optical Tomography (DOT) — a non-ionizing, low-cost neuroimaging modality that uses near-infrared light to reconstruct hemodynamic activity in the brain. DOT is uniquely suited to neonatal brain imaging because it is portable, safe at the bedside, and works through the thin neonatal skull.

The hard problems in DOT are:

  • the forward model: simulating how photons scatter through heterogeneous biological tissue, traditionally via Monte Carlo photon transport (MCX, iso2mesh, NIRFASTer);
  • the inverse problem: reconstructing absorption and scattering maps from sparse, noisy boundary measurements — an ill-posed problem that benefits from regularization (Tikhonov, GCV) and modern learned priors (diffusion posterior sampling, score-based generative models);
  • real-time performance on commodity hardware so the pipeline can run during clinical care, not after.
